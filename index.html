From a Bookstore to the Bees

An idea seeded by an old book

It didn’t start with a farm. It started with a used copy of Walden.

Found in a city bookstore — dog-eared, under a flickering light — that old pine-covered book planted an idea. That maybe we don’t need to push the land to yield. Maybe we need to step back. Let it breathe. And trust the bees to find their way.

Years later, Wild Acres Honey Farm was born from that belief. One jar at a time, one field at a time. Not a business plan, but a promise: to work with nature instead of trying to control it. We bottle small-batch honey from places left a little wild — fields full of clover, hillsides where goldenrod grows free, and gardens tended not with control, but with care.

We are not a single location. We are a cooperative, a scattered sanctuary. A collective of stewards — beekeepers, gardeners, landowners — building a future where pollinators thrive and people reconnect with the land.

What is a Wild Arce?

A promise and a practice

A Wild Arce isn’t just a plot of land — it’s a choice. A promise. A practice.

It’s a space where nature sets the pace. Where dandelions are left for the bees, clover is welcomed underfoot, and hedgerows are full of buzzing, fluttering life.

A Wild Arce might be:

  • A backyard strip blooming with bee balm and milkweed
  • A field edge left unmowed in early summer
  • A rooftop garden with native herbs in full, fragrant bloom
  • A schoolyard garden where pollinators become part of the curriculum

Each Wild Arce becomes part of our shared map. Through our Wild Arce Certification Kits™, supporters can mark their space with a field journal, a rustic sign, native wildflower seeds, and illustrated planting guides — planting a visible patch of hope in a world that needs more color.

Who is Wild Acres Honey Farm?

More than a brand

Wild Acres Honey Farm is more than a brand.

We’re a field-to-jar ecosystem, and a gentle act of resistance in a too-fast world.

We are:

  • A honey farm cooperative, where landowners host hives in exchange for honey and pollination.
  • A pollinator habitat alliance, growing a patchwork map of gardens, pastures, and quiet corners.
  • A seasonal product line, rooted in real harvests and packed with care — from beeswax lip balm and salves to food wraps and "native-to-where-you-are" seed-packets.
  • A story-sharing brand, where every jar, every farm, every bee yard has a name, and every season tells a tale.
  • A mobile tasting experience we call "The Honey Flight™," with traveling pop-ups and seasonal events where visitors can sample single-origin honeys, learn the stories of the land behind each jar, and explore our handmade goods.
  • A public education and outreach program, bringing bee-friendly gardening, habitat awareness, and hands-on learning to local schools, markets, and curious minds of all ages.
  • A community-driven conservation network, coordinating ethical bee swarm rescues and relocation through our growing map of regional beekeeper partners.
